The challenge

When middle leadership struggles, the whole school feels it.

Nearly half of middle leaders leave their role within five years. Not because they lack subject expertise, but because the move into leadership asks something different of them: the ability to manage people, navigate complexity, and act with confidence when the answers aren't clear.

For headteachers and trust leaders, this shows up as a constant drain on SLT time. Problems land on your desk that shouldn't. Training budgets are spent on courses with little lasting impact. And the cycle continues.

SLT time absorbed by issues middle leaders could resolve themselves.

Recruitment and retention costs rising as leaders burn out and leave.

Training investment that does not translate into changed practice.

Frequently asked questions.

How is coaching different from the leadership training we have already invested in?

Training delivers information to a group. Coaching works with one person on their real challenges over a sustained period. Research consistently shows that professional development needs to be ongoing to create lasting change. A one-day course may shift awareness, but it rarely changes behaviour. Coaching gives leaders space to think through live challenges, build strategies that fit their context, and develop confidence by applying them in practice.

We are under budget pressure. How do I justify this cost?

That is a valid question, and there is a practical way to look at it. The cost of one coaching programme is similar to a single day of whole-school INSET. The difference is that it is targeted at one leader, sustained over a full term, and designed to create lasting change. Compare that with the cost of a middle leader leaving: recruitment, cover, lost time, impact on team morale. Coaching has been shown to improve confidence, self-belief, problem-solving, and strategic thinking. The return is often stronger than most CPD spend.

How much time will this take out of my middle leader's timetable?

Sessions are scheduled flexibly with timetables and cover in mind. The aim is to minimise disruption while protecting enough consistency for the work to be effective. Many leaders find they recover that time quickly because fewer issues are escalated upwards. Get in touch to discuss what would work for your school.

How will I know if it is working?

This is agreed at the outset. Success looks different in every school, so the initial conversation focuses on what change you want to see and how it would show up in practice. Many headteachers notice a shift in how their middle leader handles challenges: fewer escalations, more confident decisions, more constructive conversations.

My middle leader is capable but overwhelmed. Is coaching right for them?

This is one of the most common situations. Many middle leaders are carrying more responsibility than their role suggests and have not had the support to match. Coaching is not remedial. It supports capable people by giving them space to think, a framework for their challenges, and confidence in their judgement. It starts from strength, not deficit.

Can this be done online, or does Ev need to come in?

Both options are available. Online sessions work well and are often easier to schedule consistently around the school day. In-person sessions are available for schools in London or for those who prefer it. Many international clients are supported fully online with no loss of impact.

We are an international school. Can Ev work with us?

Yes. Ev has experience in international schools as a leader, coach, and accreditor. She understands the pressures of that environment: different leadership structures, staff turnover, cultural complexity, the demands placed on middle leaders. Online delivery means location and time zones are rarely a barrier.
